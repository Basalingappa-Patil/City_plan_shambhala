Overview

Name Origin: "Shambhala" comes from the Sanskrit word "Shambu," meaning peaceful or tranquil. It is inspired by a mythical land of peace and harmony, recreated as a smart city in Gujarat.

Size & Location: Shambhala spans 1,500 sq.km (370,658 acres) in Gujarat, India. It aims to house 7 to 7.5 million residents, integrating technology, sustainability, and citizen-centric governance.

Key Goals: To create a livable, efficient urban environment while promoting economic diversity, inclusivity, and long-term sustainability.

Problem Space

The concept of Shambhala arose from the pressing challenges of urbanization, sustainability, and efficient governance. Extensive research, including a white paper analysis, highlighted the following key findings:

  • Lessons from Other Cities: The failures and successes of projects like Lavasa and Masdar have provided valuable insights. These include the importance of inclusivity, economic diversification, and environmental sustainability.
  • Urban Challenges: Rapid urban growth often leads to inadequate infrastructure, environmental degradation, and socio-economic inequality. Shambhala aims to address these by leveraging technology and smart governance.

City Design Reflection: Inspired by an eight-petaled lotus, the city is divided into modular sectors focusing on critical areas like healthcare, education, technology, and governance. This ensures balanced growth and efficient resource allocation.

Problem Definition & Team

Problem Definition: The challenge is to design a smart city that is not only technologically advanced but also socially inclusive and environmentally sustainable. It should cater to a population of 7 to 7.5 million, integrating rural and urban dynamics while ensuring long-term viability.
