Inventory Management System

×

In a thriving urban environment like Shambhala, efficient inventory management is crucial for both businesses and public services. Effective inventory management ensures that products and resources are available when needed, reducing waste and improving operational efficiency. This system is particularly important for:

  • Retail Stores: Ensuring shelves are stocked with products in high demand.
  • Healthcare Facilities: Maintaining a steady supply of medical supplies and equipment.
  • Public Services: Managing resources like emergency supplies, maintenance materials, and utilities.

Possible solution:

The problem could be addressed with a solution by using Sorting Algorithms and Searching algorithms.

Sorting Algorithms: The use of sorting algorithms, such as Quick Sort or Merge Sort, could be done to organize inventory items based on different attributes like expiration dates, stock levels, or sales frequency. By sorting the inventory efficiently, businesses and public services can easily locate and prioritize items that need immediate attention or replenishment.
Binary Search: Binary Search can be used to quickly find specific products in a sorted inventory. This ensures that items can be retrieved rapidly, reducing wait times and improving overall operational efficiency.

Brute Force Search: Brute Force Search can be used to sequentially check each item in the inventory to find specific products. This straightforward method ensures that every element is examined until the desired item is found.

This could then be integrated as a whole to present as an efficient method to address the problem.

Related SDG's

SDG 11.6 - Reduce Environmental Impact By 2030, reduce the adverse per capita environmental impact of cities, including by paying special attention to air quality and municipal and other waste management.

  • It focuses on minimizing Waste using sorting algorithms to prioritize perishable goods and high-demand items, businesses can reduce waste due to expired or unsold products.

4. Fraud Detection in Utility Billing Records


×

Problem: In the Shambhala Smart City, utility billing systems handle massive data for electricity, water, and waste management services. Duplicate or fraudulent entries in billing records can lead to financial losses and inefficiencies. Detecting such patterns quickly and accurately is essential to ensure transparency and reliability in governance. Solution:
The Rabin-Karp algorithm identifies duplicates efficiently using hash-based comparisons, avoiding a brute-force approach. Handles large datasets of utility records due to its hash-based optimization. Improves transparency and reliability in the utility management system, aligning with SDG 11 goals of sustainable and efficient governance.
Related SDG:
SDG 11.1: Ensure access to adequate, safe, and affordable housing and basic services and Utilize population data to forecast housing needs. Ensure housing affordability for low-income families.
Target 11.3 Enhance inclusive and sustainable urbanization: Demographic analysis supports participatory urban planning and management.
Objective: :To identify duplicate or fraudulent billing entries across the utility database using the Rabin-Karp Algorithm for efficient multi-pattern string searching.
