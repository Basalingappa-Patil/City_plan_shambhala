Business Cases

Occurence of Drought

×

Since the city is located in the state of Gujarat, there is a possibility of having periodic droughts which could affect several factors of the city like Agricultural productivity, Industrial area, Wildlife sanctuary. This could result as a long term challenge since the water supply is a major problem in the city.

Possible solution: The problem could be addressed with a solution by using Divide and Conquer technique and BFS.

Divide and Conquer: Divide the region into smaller zones, which has its own water needs and sources.

Solve the problem with techniques like BFS which finds the shortest path by which the source of water supply can be used appropriately.

This could then be integrated as a whole to present as an efficient method to address the problem.

Related SDG's

SDG 6 - Clean Water and Sanitation: This goal focuses on ensuring availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all. Addressing drought and optimizing water supply directly aligns with improving access to clean water and promoting sustainable water management.

SDG 11 - Sustainable Cities and Communities: Ensuring sustainable water management and addressing the challenges of drought contribute to making cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable.

Public Transportation Management

×

The level of designing the city has been done to an extent where maximum of the facilities are affordable, feasible and the city aims that the people live with peace and harmony (as the name suggests). As a result, there are various facilities that could be used and they actually prove to be a necessary factor in the overall development of the city. Transportation is one such facility that everyone is in need of. So, planning proper placement of stations, stops, and parking areas is a crucial step for seamless connectivity. This could lead to several other factors like traffic management, efficient routes, etc.

Possible solution:

The problem could be addressed with a solution by using Divide and Conquer technique and BFS.

Graph algorithms: The use of graph algorithms could be done to model the city’s infrastructure and to analyze traffic patterns. BFS could be used to determine the best locations for transportation hubs.

This could then be integrated as a whole to present as an efficient method to address the problem.

Related SDG's

SDG 8 - Decent Work and Economic Growth: Efficient public transportation facilitates access to employment opportunities, education, and services, thereby promoting inclusive and sustainable economic growth, productive employment, and decent work for all.

SDG 11 - Sustainable Cities and Communities: This goal focuses on making cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable. Efficient public transportation systems contribute to reducing traffic congestion, improving air quality, and enhancing overall urban mobility, making cities more sustainable.

Waste Management and Recycling

×

Problem:
Segregation and routing of waste from households to recycling centers, minimizing operational costs and environmental impact.

Solution:
•Algorithm: Implement a sorting algorithm like Merge Sort to classify waste based on recyclability and contamination levels.
•Engineering Implementation: Use IoT sensors in bins to categorize waste and prioritize pick-up routes. Optimize waste collection routes using the Travelling Salesman Problem (TSP) approach.

Related SDG:
Goal 12 - Responsible Consumption and Production, by promoting efficient waste management systems.

Renewable Energy Grid Optimization

×

Problem:
Efficiently distributing solar and wind-generated energy across residential, industrial, and public zones.
Solution:
•Algorithm:Use Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) to optimize the energy grid infrastructure and distribution.
•Engineering Implementation:Model energy connections as a graph with energy demand as weights. Deploy Kruskal’s algorithm to design the most cost-effective grid. Use dynamic programming to adapt grid optimization based on energy consumption patterns.
Related SDG:
Goal 7 - Affordable and Clean Energy, by ensuring efficient and sustainable energy distribution.

 Optimizing Public Transport Routes

×

Problem:
Efficiently connecting 10 taluks using public transportation, minimizing travel time for 7.5 million residents.
Solution:
• Algorithm: Apply Dijkstra’s algorithm for finding the shortest path between transportation hubs. Use graph traversal to map roads and calculate optimal routes.
•Engineering Implementation:
Represent the city as a weighted graph where nodes are hubs and edges are road distances.
Prioritize high-demand routes using population data from different areas.
Use Breadth-First Search (BFS) for exploring potential alternative routes during emergencies.
Related SDG:
Goal 11 - Sustainable Cities and Communities, by ensuring accessible, efficient, and sustainable public transportation.

Water Supply Management

×

Problem: Distributing water efficiently while preventing wastage in different city zones.
Solution:
•Algorithm: Depth-First Search (DFS) to detect leakages and monitor pipelines, and Quick Sort for prioritizing repairs based on severity.
•Engineering Implementation: Use smart meters to collect data on water flow. Implement algorithms for real-time pipeline monitoring and repair scheduling.
Related SDG:
Goal 6 - Clean Water and Sanitation, by improving water efficiency and reducing wastage.

Emergency Services Deployment

×

Problem: Rapid response to emergencies (e.g., healthcare, fire incidents) in densely populated areas.
Solution:
•Algorithm: A Search Algorithm to find the fastest routes from emergency hubs to incident locations.
•Engineering Implementation: Represent the city layout as a grid with heuristic-based weights for road traffic and distances. Integrate real-time traffic data for dynamic route adjustments.
Related SDG:
Goal 3 - Good Health and Well-being, by ensuring timely access to emergency services.

Smart Traffic Management System

×

Problem:Managing traffic congestion across the city's transportation infrastructure, especially during peak hours in urban and residential zones.
Solution:
•Algorithm: Use Greedy Algorithms for dynamic signal control and A Search Algorithm* for route optimization based on real-time traffic data. This system not only improves efficiency but also reduces environmental impact, ensuring sustainable urban mobility.
•Engineering Implementation: Traffic signals can be optimized using greedy algorithms that prioritize traffic flow based on congestion levels at intersections. A search can be applied to help commuters navigate the shortest and least congested paths. Use real-time data from traffic sensors and IoT devices to update traffic signals and guide vehicles efficiently.
Related SDG:
Goal 9 - Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure, by enhancing transportation infrastructure with smart technologies to reduce congestion and emissions. services.
