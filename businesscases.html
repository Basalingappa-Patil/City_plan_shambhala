3. Water connectivity and Sanitation

source : freepik.com

One in three people live without sanitation. This is causing unnecessary disease and death. Although huge strides have been made with access to clean drinking water, lack of sanitation is undermining these advances. If we provide affordable equipment and education in hygiene practices, we can stop this senseless suffering and loss of life.

  • SDG 6 : clean water and sanitation Everyone can help to make sure that we meet the Global Goals. Use these eight targets to ensure clean water and sanitation for all..

    SDG 6 has eight targets including two so-called "implementing targets". Six of them are to be achieved by the year 2030, one by the year 2020, and one has no target year.[12] Each of the targets also has one or two indicators which will be used to measure progress. In total there are 11 indicators to monitor progress for SDG6.[13] The main data sources for the SDG 6 targets and indicators come from the Integrated Monitoring Initiative for SDG 6 coordinated by UN-Water.[4] The six "outcome-oriented targets" include: Safe and affordable drinking water; end open defecation and provide access to sanitation, and hygiene, improve water quality, wastewater treatment and safe reuse, increase water-use efficiency and ensure freshwater supplies, implement IWRM, protect and restore water-related ecosystems. The two "means of achieving" targets are to expand water and sanitation support to developing countries, and to support local engagement in water and sanitation management.[4].

  • SDG 13 : Climate action take immediate action to address environmental challenges.

    SDG 13, which emphasizes urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts, is closely connected to the green urban mobility plans of Vatsalya Nagar. By transitioning to electric buses, bike-sharing programs, and pedestrian-friendly pathways, the city reduces greenhouse gas emissions and minimizes its carbon footprint. These eco-friendly transportation solutions align with Vatsalya's commitment to achieving carbon neutrality by 2050. Additionally, integrating renewable energy into vehicle charging infrastructure further reinforces the city’s climate action goals. Promoting green urban mobility not only mitigates climate risks but also creates a sustainable urban environment that exemplifies proactive climate leadership while improving the quality of life for its residents.

  • SDG 3 : Good health and well being ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all.

    SDG 3, which aims to ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all, is strongly linked to the green urban mobility initiatives of Vatsalya Nagar. By introducing electric buses, bike-sharing systems, and pedestrian-friendly pathways, the city significantly reduces air pollution caused by conventional vehicles, leading to better respiratory and cardiovascular health for residents. Encouraging walking and cycling as part of daily commutes promotes physical fitness and mental well-being. Additionally, safer and greener transportation infrastructure reduces traffic-related accidents and fosters a healthier urban environment. Through these initiatives, Vatsalya directly enhances the health and well-being of its citizens while contributing to a more sustainable and livable city.
